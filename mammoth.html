The Legendary Runs

Ten trails that define Mammoth Mountain

1
Cornice Bowl Expert
Steep chutes and wide-open faces above treeline. This is Mammoth's crown jewel for expert skiers chasing big mountain terrain and Sierra sunrises.
2
Climax Intermediate
Wide-open groomer perfect for high-speed cruising. Named for the adrenaline rush, this run epitomizes Sierra spring skiing at its finest.
3
Dave's Run Intermediate
Classic Mammoth cruiser with varied terrain and stunning views. A local favorite that showcases everything great about Sierra skiing.
4
Wipeout Chutes Expert
Technical chutes off the summit that demand respect. When fresh snow fills these steep lines, powder hounds line up at first chair.
5
Broadway Intermediate
Long, flowing run through the trees with consistent pitch. Perfect for warming up legs or cooling down after hard charging.
6
Gravy Train Intermediate
Smooth, confidence-building terrain that lives up to its name. Ideal for progressing skiers ready to tackle longer runs.
7
Hangman's Hollow Advanced
Steep, technical tree skiing for advanced riders. When the snow is deep, this hidden gem offers some of the best tree runs on the mountain.
8
St. Anton Advanced
European-style steeps named after the legendary Austrian resort. This run pays homage to classic Alpine skiing with sustained pitch and fall-line skiing.
9
Scotty's Intermediate
Cruisy intermediate with great views of the Minarets. Named after a Mammoth legend, this run captures the soul of California skiing.
10
Face of 3 Expert
Steep, sustained pitch directly under Chair 3. A proving ground for confident skiers and a spectator gallery for those riding the lift.

Frequently Asked Questions

When is the best time to ski Mammoth? ‚ñº
February through April offers the best combination of deep snowpack and longer days. December and January can be colder with shorter days, but offer excellent early-season conditions. The resort typically operates into May or June depending on snowpack.
How crowded is Mammoth? ‚ñº
Mammoth gets busy on weekends and holidays, especially from LA. Weekdays are significantly less crowded. The vast terrain (3,500+ acres) helps distribute crowds. Arrive early (before 8am) for best parking and first tracks.
Where can I park overnight? ‚ñº
Official overnight parking is available at Main Lodge ($25/night) and Canyon Lodge ($20/night). Nearby free options include Convict Lake, Sherwin Creek Road (BLM land), and various dispersed camping spots along Lake Mary Road. Always check current regulations and fire restrictions.
Is Mammoth vanlife-friendly? ‚ñº
Yes! Mammoth is one of the most vanlife-friendly ski resorts. Multiple overnight parking options, dump stations at Main Lodge, water fill stations, and diesel fuel available in town. Winter temps can drop to 0¬∞F, so prepare with proper insulation and heating.
Is Mammoth good for beginners? ‚ñº
Absolutely. 25% of terrain is beginner-friendly with wide, groomed runs. Discovery Chair and Schoolyard Express offer excellent learning terrain. The resort has multiple ski schools with group and private lessons. Broadway area is perfect for building confidence before progressing.
Does Mammoth have good terrain parks? ‚ñº
Yes! Unbound Terrain Parks at Mammoth are world-class with features for all levels. Main Park has jumps and rails, Jibs Park focuses on technical features, and South Park offers progression terrain. Parks are typically open late November through April.
